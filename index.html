About Me

I am a Master's student in Marketing at The University of Texas at Dallas, with a deep passion for understanding how humans perceive and interact with chemical stimuli. My work bridges consumer psychology, sensory science, and engineering.

Currently, I collaborate with Professor Jas Brooks at MIT EECS & CSAIL on perceptual engineering research, developing electronic tongue technology for chemical safety applications. This work combines sensor design, machine learning, and psychophysical methods to create systems that augment human chemical perception.

My journey into HCI began with founding PerVenture, a startup exploring VR-based olfactory interfaces for fragrance shopping. The technical challenges I encountered there—particularly the gap between digital and chemical modalities—motivated me to pursue research at the frontier of perceptual engineering.

I am seeking PhD positions where I can investigate olfactory perception, chemical interfaces, and multisensory HCI—designing systems that let people sense, understand, and interact with the chemical world in new ways.

Chemical Safety via Electronic Tongue Technology

MIT EECS & CSAIL | Advisor: Prof. Jas Brooks | Apr 2025 – Present

Developing an electronic tongue system to prevent hazardous chemical interactions between household cleaning products. When common products like bleach and toilet bowl cleaner are mixed, they can produce toxic chlorine gas—our system detects and warns users before dangerous combinations occur.

  • Built a comprehensive dataset of 44 cleaning products with multiple ion-selective electrode measurements for training a chemical identification system
  • Designed and implemented sensor array circuitry using Arduino-based microcontrollers interfaced with ion-selective electrodes for real-time chemical detection
  • Applied dimensionality reduction techniques (PCA, t-SNE) to visualize chemical signatures and identify discriminative markers across surfactant-based products

National Science Council, Taiwan | Jul 2023 – Mar 2024
